# UTS Master of Information Technology

## Overview
> Source: https://www.uts.edu.au/study/find-a-course/master-information-technology

The Master of Information Technology is designed for professionals seeking to advance their careers through specialised technical or management areas of IT. Students gain comprehensive understanding of IT within commercial contexts and develop skills for roles including business intelligence experts, e-business developers, games developers, information systems managers, IT security analysts, and software architects.

- **UTS Course Code:** C04295 (version 3)
- **CRICOS Code:** 084256C
- **Professional Recognition:** Eligible for professional-level membership with the Australian Computer Society (ACS)
- **Location:** City campus

## Admission Requirements
> Source: https://www.uts.edu.au/study/find-a-course/master-information-technology

Applicants must meet **one** of the following:

1. Completed an Australian master's or doctoral degree (or overseas equivalent)
2. Completed an Australian bachelor's degree, graduate certificate, or graduate diploma with ≥75% of subjects passed at pass level
3. Completed a bachelor's or graduate qualification **plus** a minimum of 2 years full-time equivalent professional post-secondary work experience

**Supporting documentation required for work experience claims (any one of):**
- Curriculum Vitae
- Statement of Service (employer letter, UTS form, statutory declaration, accountant/solicitor letter, or business registration certificate)

**Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL):**
- Students may be eligible for up to 24 credit points RPL
- Undergraduate IT degree holders may qualify for up to 24 CP RPL
- Subjects must typically have been completed within 10 years prior to course commencement
- See: https://www.uts.edu.au/for-students/admissions-entry/recognition-of-prior-learning

## Non-IT Background Applicants
> Source: https://www.uts.edu.au/study/find-a-course/master-information-technology

- Admission condition 3 explicitly accepts bachelor's degrees **in any field of study**, combined with a minimum of 2 years full-time equivalent post-secondary professional work experience
- No prerequisite IT subjects are required for entry
- Applicants who do not meet any of the three admission conditions should consider applying for **C11247 Graduate Certificate in Information Technology Studies** first; upon completion, up to 24 credit points of RPL may be granted into the Master of Information Technology


## Post-graduation Migration Options
> Source: https://www.uts.edu.au/study/find-a-course/master-information-technology  
> Source: https://www.acs.org.au/cpd-education/accredited-courses.html
> Source: https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/getting-a-visa/visa-listing/temporary-graduate-485/

- Graduates of this 2-year full-time master's degree are eligible to apply for the **Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485)**
- 485 visa validity:
  - Study in a regional area: up to 4 years (2 + 2 years)
  - Study in a major city (Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane): 2 years
- UTS MIT (C04295) is listed on the **Australian Computer Society (ACS) Accredited Courses** list
- Graduates may apply for ACS Professional Membership upon completion
- ACS accredited courses list: https://www.acs.org.au/cpd-education/accredited-courses.html
