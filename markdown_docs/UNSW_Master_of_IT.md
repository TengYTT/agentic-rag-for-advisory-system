# UNSW Master of Information Technology

## Overview
> Source: https://www.unsw.edu.au/study/postgraduate/master-of-information-technology

The Master of Information Technology is a two-year full-time postgraduate degree that prepares you to make a difference through technology. It is open to both students with computing backgrounds and those without prior IT experience.

- **UNSW Program Code:** 8543
- **CRICOS Code:** 061294J
- **Professional Recognition:** Accredited by the Australian Computer Society (ACS) at Professional level
- **Location:** Kensington campus, Sydney
- **Delivery:** Face-to-face (includes blended)
- **Term system:** UNSW 3+ Trimester system (Term 1, Term 2, Term 3) — three intakes per year


## Course Structure / Duration
> Source: https://www.unsw.edu.au/study/postgraduate/master-of-information-technology  
> Source: https://www.handbook.unsw.edu.au/postgraduate/programs/2026/8543

**Total credit points:** 96 UoC (Units of Credit)

Duration varies by entry point and prior qualification:

| Entry Point | Background | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Cognate (standard) | Bachelor in cognate discipline | 2 years full-time |
| Cognate (accelerated) | 3-year cognate bachelor | 1.7 years |
| Cognate (accelerated) | 4-year honours cognate bachelor | 1 year |
| Non-Cognate (packaged) | Bachelor in any discipline | 2 years full-time (incl. Graduate Certificate in IT) |

**Year 1 — Core courses:**
- Principles of Programming
- Data Structures & Algorithms
- Database Systems
- Computer Networks & Applications
- Project Management

Students also begin their chosen specialisation courses in Year 1.

**Year 2 — Advanced study:**
- Continuation of specialisation courses
- Computing and non-computing electives

**Capstone (choose one):**
- Information Technology Project: team-based definition, implementation, and evaluation of a real-world software system
- Research Project: two-stage research project completed under academic supervision

> For full course listing see: https://www.handbook.unsw.edu.au/postgraduate/programs/2026/8543

### UNSW 3+ Trimester System

## Admission Requirements
> Source: https://www.unsw.edu.au/study/postgraduate/master-of-information-technology

UNSW provides **two entry points** to the Master of IT depending on your cognate background. Both entry points equate to the same duration of 2 years (96 UoC), and differ only in the order and structure of core foundation courses.

> Note: While a background in maths is not required for either entry, it is strongly recommended for all Master of IT students.

---

### Entry Point 1: Cognate Background

**Requirement:**
A bachelor degree (AQF Level 7 equivalent) in a cognate discipline with a minimum weighted average mark of 65%*

**Cognate disciplines include:**
Artificial Intelligence, Deep learning, Machine Learning, Mechatronics, Autonomous systems, Computer Science, Information Technology, Software Engineering, Computer Engineering, Computer Science and Statistics, Data Analytics, Database management, Internet of Things, Network Engineering, Remote Sensing, Electronic Information, Communication Technology, Network Security, Cyber Security, Information Security, Management Information Systems, Technology, Engineering

**Accelerated entry options (cognate background only):**
- **1-year completion:** 4-year honours bachelor degree (AQF Level 8) in a cognate discipline
- **1.7-year completion:** 3-year bachelor degree (AQF Level 7) in a cognate discipline

---

### Entry Point 2: Non-Cognate Background (Packaged Offer)

**Requirement:**
A 3-year bachelor degree (AQF Level 7 equivalent) in **any discipline** with a minimum 65% average*

**How it works:**
- Successful applicants receive a packaged offer: Graduate Certificate in IT (18 UoC) + Master of IT
- No separate application required; the system assesses background automatically upon applying to the MIT (8543)
- After completing the 18 UoC Graduate Certificate foundation courses, students transition directly into the Master of IT
- Total duration remains 2 years (96 UoC), same as cognate entry
- A single Student Visa (subclass 500) covers the entire 2-year packaged program

---

*\* Applicants from Chinese universities outside the 211 scheme require a minimum 70% average.*

## Non-IT Background Applicants
> Source: https://www.unsw.edu.au/study/postgraduate/master-of-information-technology

- UNSW MIT is open to applicants **without a computing background**
- Non-cognate applicants enter via the **packaged offer path**: Graduate Certificate in IT (18 UoC) + Master of IT
- Apply directly to MIT (program code 8543); no separate Graduate Certificate application required
- Total duration is the same as cognate students: 2 years (96 UoC)
- Student Visa (subclass 500) covers the full 2-year packaged program in a single application
- Applicants who do not meet standard entry requirements may consider applying for the **Graduate Certificate in Computing** independently, then applying to the MIT separately


## Post-graduation Migration Options
> Source: https://www.unsw.edu.au/study/postgraduate/master-of-information-technology  
> Source: https://www.acs.org.au/cpd-education/accredited-courses.html

- Graduates of this 2-year full-time master's degree are eligible to apply for the **Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485)**
- 485 visa validity:
  - Study in a regional area: up to 4 years (2 + 2 years)
  - Study in a major city (Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane): 2 years
- UNSW MIT (8543) is listed on the **Australian Computer Society (ACS) Accredited Courses** list at Professional level
- Graduates may apply for ACS Professional Membership upon completion
- ACS accredited courses list: https://www.acs.org.au/cpd-education/accredited-courses.html
